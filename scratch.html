My Reading Life

Why I Read

Books have shaped how I think about everything — money, people, history, and the future. Growing up and especially during my two years in Sweden, reading became a discipline and a refuge. There's something irreplaceable about sitting with a book and letting someone else's ideas completely rewire your perspective.

My reading spans personal finance, behavioral economics, history, biography, and the occasional novel. I keep track of everything I've read and want to read — this page is part catalog, part manifesto.

If a book changed how I see money or decision-making, it belongs on this list.

My kind of weekend.

"The more that you read, the more things you will know. The more that you learn, the more places you'll go." — Dr. Seuss

Reading Categories

I organize my reading into a few major categories. Here's a breakdown of the genres and some standout titles I've read or am reading within each:

  1. Personal Finance & Investing
    • The Psychology of Money — Morgan Housel
    • The Intelligent Investor — Benjamin Graham
    • Rich Dad Poor Dad — Robert Kiyosaki
    • A Random Walk Down Wall Street — Burton Malkiel
  2. Behavioral Economics & Decision Making
    • Thinking, Fast and Slow — Daniel Kahneman
    • Predictably Irrational — Dan Ariely
    • Nudge — Thaler & Sunstein
  3. History & Biography
    • Shoe Dog — Phil Knight
    • The Snowball (Warren Buffett) — Alice Schroeder
    • Steve Jobs — Walter Isaacson
    • Team of Rivals — Doris Kearns Goodwin
  4. Self-Development & Leadership
    • Atomic Habits — James Clear
    • Deep Work — Cal Newport
    • Man's Search for Meaning — Viktor Frankl
  5. Fiction & Literature
    • Crime and Punishment — Fyodor Dostoevsky
    • The Brothers Karamazov — Fyodor Dostoevsky
    • Ender's Game — Orson Scott Card
